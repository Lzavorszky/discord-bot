# AMPICILLIN_SULBACTAM_HIGH_DOSE

## PROTOCOL_NAME

High-dose ampicillin/sulbactam protocol for multidrug-resistant Acinetobacter infections (MACI/CRAB)

---

## ALIASES

- ampicillin/sulbactam
- AMP/SUL
- sulbactam high-dose
- high-dose sulbactam
- CRAB protocol
- MACI protocol

---

## DRUG_CLASS

- beta-lactam
- beta-lactam/beta-lactamase inhibitor

---

## ANSWER_POLICY

Can default dosing be given if some information is missing?
- no

High-dose ampicillin/sulbactam dosing depends strongly on:
- renal function
- CRRT/IHD status
- confirmed MACI/CRAB infection

Do NOT provide dosing before required information is available.

Do NOT provide empiric/default dosing.

Do NOT list all dosing tiers unless explicitly requested.

Instead:
- ask for the required information first
- then provide the matching dosing recommendation.

---

## REQUIRED_INFORMATION

Required before dosing:
- current renal function/GFR
- CRRT or intermittent hemodialysis (IHD)?
- is this confirmed MACI/CRAB infection?

Preferably clarify:
- bloodstream infection present?

If renal function not provided:
- ask for GFR/eGFR
- ask if CRRT or IHD present

If resistance confirmation unclear:
- ask whether Acinetobacter is carbapenem resistant (CRAB/MACI)

Do NOT provide dosing before required information is available.

---

## PREFERRED_INFORMATION

After providing dosing, clarify when relevant:
- sulbactam inhibition zone?

---

## MODIFIER_INFORMATION

These may modify treatment interpretation:
- CRRT
- IHD
- severe renal dysfunction
- bloodstream infection
- sulbactam inhibition zone ≤11 mm
- tigecycline MIC
- confirmed carbapenem resistance
---

## INDICATIONS

Use only for:
- confirmed multidrug-resistant Acinetobacter infection (MACI)
- confirmed carbapenem-resistant Acinetobacter infection (CRAB)

Particularly:
- bloodstream infection
- severe ICU infection
- ventilator-associated pneumonia
- severe nosocomial pneumonia

Usually used:
- as combination therapy
- together with another active agent based on antibiogram

---

## PRIMARY_DOSING_AND_ADMINISTRATION

### DEFAULT_EMPIRIC_ICU_DOSING

If renal function unknown:
- Sulbactam: 9 g/day
- Ampicillin: 18 g/day

Administration:
- continuous infusion preferred
- dissolve total daily dose in 250 mL NaCl 0.9%
- administer via volumetric pump
- infusion rate: 11 mL/h

After default dosing:
- ask for renal function/GFR
- ask for CRRT/IHD status

---

### GFR ≥60

For confirmed MACI/CRAB:
- Sulbactam: 9 g/day
- Ampicillin: 18 g/day

Administration:
- continuous infusion preferred
- dissolve total daily dose in 250 mL NaCl 0.9%
- administer via volumetric pump
- infusion rate: 11 mL/h

---

### GFR 30–60

For confirmed MACI/CRAB:
- Sulbactam: 6 g/day
- Ampicillin: 12 g/day

Administration:
- continuous infusion preferred
- dissolve total daily dose in 250 mL NaCl 0.9%
- volumetric pump recommended
- infusion rate may require local adjustment

---

### GFR 15–30

For confirmed MACI/CRAB:
- Sulbactam: 3 g/day
- Ampicillin: 6 g/day

Administration:
- continuous infusion preferred
- individualized administration may be required

---

### GFR <15

For confirmed MACI/CRAB:
- Sulbactam: 2 g/day
- Ampicillin: 4 g/day

Administration:
- bolus/intermittent dosing may be preferred

---

### CRRT

For confirmed MACI/CRAB:
- Sulbactam: 9 g/day
- Ampicillin: 18 g/day

Administration:
- continuous infusion preferred
- dissolve total daily dose in 250 mL NaCl 0.9%
- administer via volumetric pump
- infusion rate: 11 mL/h

---

### IHD

For confirmed MACI/CRAB:
- Sulbactam: 1.5 g/day
- Ampicillin: 3 g/day

Administration:
- bolus/intermittent dosing may be preferred

---

## FOLLOW_UP_QUESTIONS

After providing dosing guidance, ask remaining relevant clarification questions:
- current renal function/GFR?
- CRRT or IHD?
- confirmed MACI/CRAB?
- bloodstream infection?
- confirmed infection vs colonization?
- sulbactam inhibition zone?
- tigecycline MIC?

Do NOT stop after giving partial/default dosing.

---

## MICROBIOLOGY_REQUIREMENTS

Sulbactam activity considered favorable when:
- inhibition zone >11 mm

Interpretation:
- inhibition zone >11 mm:
  - sulbactam potentially useful
- inhibition zone ≤11 mm:
  - sulbactam efficacy questionable

Tigecycline:
- MIC <2 mg/L considered favorable

Conventional susceptibility testing remains definitive.

---

## COMBINATION_THERAPY

Recommended approach:
- use TWO active agents

One agent:
- sulbactam (if inhibition zone >11 mm)

Second agent:
- based on antibiogram

Possible examples:
- colistin
- tigecycline
- cefiderocol
- others according to susceptibility

Avoid monotherapy in severe infection.

---

## PK_PD_TARGETS

Dose optimized for:
- 60% fT > MIC
- 90% target attainment

Assumed MIC:
- 32 mg/L

---

## TREATMENT_DURATION

Typical duration:
- 7–14 days

Depends on:
- source control
- bacteremia persistence
- microbiological clearance
- clinical response

---

## IMPORTANT_COMMENTS

- Continuous infusion preferred in ICU patients
- Primarily intended for severe ICU infections
- Particularly intended for bloodstream infection due to resistant Acinetobacter
- Early microbiology consultation recommended
- Infectious diseases consultation strongly advised
- Monitor renal function closely
- Monitor microbiological response carefully

---

## ESCALATION_AND_WARNINGS

Escalate to infectious diseases / microbiology consultation if:
- persistent bacteremia
- worsening shock
- unclear susceptibility pattern
- sulbactam inhibition zone ≤11 mm
- treatment failure
- severe renal dysfunction
- unclear distinction between colonization and infection

---

## COMMENTS

This protocol is intended for severe multidrug-resistant Acinetobacter infections and should generally be interpreted together with local microbiology and infectious diseases consultation.
