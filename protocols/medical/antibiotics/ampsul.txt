```markdown
# AMPICILLIN_SULBACTAM_HIGH_DOSE

## ALIASES

- ampicillin/sulbactam
- AMP/SUL
- sulbactam high-dose

---

## INDICATIONS

Used only of:
- MACI (multidrug-resistant Acinetobacter) bloodstream infection
- this dosing is exclusively for carbapenem resistant Acinetobacter infections (MACI/CRAB)
- unless explicitly stated in the conversation ask if sulbactam inhibition zone is available, suggest using it only if inhibition zone larger than 11mm
- unless explicitly stated in the conversation ask if blood cultures are positive for Acinetobacter spp. that is carbapenem resistant (CRAB/MACI)
- unless blood cultures are positive for CRAB ask if infection is certain/confirmed, suggest using it only for confirmed MACI infections 
- suggest using it in a combination with an other active agent based on antibiogram (e.g. colistin, tigecyclin)

Usually used:
- as combination therapy
- together with another active agent based on antibiogram

---

## SPECTRUM


Sulbactam activity is considered clinically useful when:
- inhibition zone >11 mm

---

## DOSING_PHILOSOPHY

- Continuous infusion preferred
- Dose optimized for PK/PD target:
  - 60% fT > MIC
  - 90% attainment
  - assuming MIC 32 mg/L

Evidence quality for severe renal dysfunction dosing:
- low

---

## CONTINUOUS_INFUSION

Standard preparation:
- dissolve total daily dose in 250 mL NaCl 0.9%
- administer via volumetric pump
- infusion rate: 11 mL/h

Bolus administration:
- possible in:
  - GFR <15
  - IHD

---

## GFR_BASED_DOSING

| GFR | Sulbactam | Ampicillin |
|---|---|---|
| 60+ | 9 g/day | 18 g/day |
| 30–60 | 6 g/day | 12 g/day |
| 15–30 | 3 g/day | 6 g/day |
| <15 | 2 g/day | 4 g/day |
| CRRT | 9 g/day | 18 g/day |
| IHD | 1.5 g/day | 3 g/day |

---

## MICROBIOLOGY_NOTES

At treatment initiation:
- request:
  - sulbactam inhibition zone
  - tigecycline MIC

Interpretation:
- tigecycline MIC <2 mg/L considered favorable
- sulbactam inhibition zone >11 mm considered favorable

If inhibition zone <11 mm:
- sulbactam efficacy questionable

---

## COMBINATION_THERAPY

Treat with:
- TWO active agents

One should be:
- sulbactam (if inhibition zone >11 mm)

Second agent:
- any active agent based on antibiogram

Possible examples:
- colistin
- tigecycline
- cefiderocol
- others according to susceptibility

---

## TREATMENT_DURATION

Typical duration:
- 7–14 days

Depends on:
- source control
- bacteremia persistence
- clinical response

---

## PK_PD_TARGETS

Target:
- 60% fT > MIC

Assumed MIC:
- 32 mg/L

---

## TOXICITY_AND_WARNINGS

- Evidence base limited in severe renal dysfunction
- Monitor renal function closely
- Monitor microbiological response carefully
- Avoid monotherapy in severe MACI infection

---

## ICU_NOTES

- Primarily intended for severe ICU infections
- Particularly bloodstream infection due to resistant Acinetobacter
- Early microbiology consultation recommended
- Infectious diseases consultation strongly advised

---

## COMMENTS

This protocol is intended for severe multidrug-resistant Acinetobacter infections and should generally be interpreted together with local microbiology and infectious diseases consultation.
```
