# MEROPENEM

## PROTOCOL_NAME

Meropenem continuous infusion ICU dosing protocol

---

## ALIASES

- meropenem
- mero
- MEM
- meronem

---

## DRUG_CLASS

- beta-lactam
- carbapenem

---

## ANSWER_POLICY

Can default dosing be given if some information is missing?
- yes

Default empiric dosing if indication and/or renal function missing:
- MAGAS dosing

Label this as:
- high-dose empiric ICU dosing
- not final individualized dosing

Do NOT refuse dosing solely because:
- obesity present
- indication not yet specified
- renal function not yet specified

Do NOT list all dosing tiers unless explicitly requested.

---

## REQUIRED_INFORMATION

No absolutely required information before giving default empiric dosing.

---

## PREFERRED_INFORMATION

After giving default or partial dosing, ask for:
- indication/infection type
- renal function/GFR
- CRRT/IHD
- ARC suspicion
- CNS infection
- pathogen/MIC
- TDM availability

---

## MODIFIER_INFORMATION

These may modify dosing tier:
- septic shock
- ARC
- CNS infection
- CRRT
- IHD
- severe AKI
- TDM result
- pathogen MIC

---

## INDICATIONS

Common ICU indications:
- severe sepsis
- septic shock
- nosocomial pneumonia
- HAP/VAP
- ESBL-producing Enterobacterales
- severe intraabdominal infection
- CNS infection
- empiric ICU therapy
- AmpC-risk Enterobacter infections
- resistant gram-negative infections

---

## SPECTRUM

Broad-spectrum carbapenem with activity against:
- Enterobacterales
- ESBL-producing organisms
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- anaerobes
- many gram-positive organisms

Limited or unreliable against:
- MRSA
- VRE
- some carbapenem-resistant organisms
- MACI

---

## DOSING_PHILOSOPHY

- Continuous infusion preferred
- PK/PD-driven dosing
- Full-dose therapy recommended in early septic shock
- Higher doses may be required in:
  - ARC
  - CNS infection
  - severe infections

---

## PATHWAY_SELECTION

### PLUSZ

Used for:
- CNS infections
- persistent subtherapeutic levels after TDM
- step-up dosing

Dose:
- 6 g/day

Administration:
- continuous infusion over 24h
- 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
- pump: 12.5 mL/h

---

### MAGAS

Used for:
- septic shock empiric therapy
- first 24 hours
- suspected ARC
- GFR >90 until TDM and pathogen MIC available

Dose:
- 4 g/day

Administration:
- continuous infusion over 24h
- 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
- pump: 8.4 mL/h

---

### ÁTLAGOS

Used for:
- standard ICU infections
- GFR 20–90
- clinically stable patients without ARC/shock

Dose:
- 3 g/day

Administration:
- continuous infusion over 24h
- 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
- pump: 6.3 mL/h

---

### CSÖKKENTETT

Used for:
- GFR <20
- severe renal dysfunction without CRRT

Dose:
- 1 g/day
OR
- 0.5 g q12h

Administration:
- continuous infusion possible
- reduced-dose regimens may require modified preparation

Typical pump:
- 4.2 mL/h

---

### CRRT

Used for:
- CRRT
- standard effluent rate <40 mL/kg/h

Dose:
- 3 g/day

Administration:
- continuous infusion over 24h
- 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
- pump: 6.3 mL/h

---

### IHD

Used for:
- intermittent hemodialysis

Dose:
- 0.5 g q12h

Administration:
- individualized consideration
- intermittent dosing may be preferred

TDM strongly encouraged.

---

## UNDER_SPECIFIED_DOSING_REQUESTS

If the user asks:
- generic meropenem dosing
- dosing for obesity
- dosing without indication
- dosing without renal function

Do NOT list all dosing tiers.

Instead:
- provide MAGAS dosing as high-dose empiric ICU dosing
- then ask for:
  - indication/infection type
  - renal function/GFR
  - CRRT/IHD
  - septic shock
  - ARC suspicion

If the user insists on generic/default dosing:
- provide ÁTLAGOS dosing.

---

## TDM

### AVAILABILITY

- external laboratory
- Semmelweis University

---

### SAMPLING

- send morning sample
- sample during continuous infusion

---

### TDM_INDICATIONS

Routine TDM:
- NOT generally required

Recommended when:
- ARC suspected
- GFR >90 with urine output >2000 mL/day
- severe AKI
- IHD
- CRRT
- difficult-to-control infection
- poor clinical response
- infective endocarditis
- abscess systems
- persistent infection

---

## PK_PD_TARGETS

Target:
- concentration above pathogen MIC

If MIC unknown:
- >8 mg/L generally acceptable

---

## TOXICITY

Known toxic threshold:
- not fully established

Generally considered safe:
- <45 mg/L

If >45 mg/L:
- dose reduction recommended

---

## IMPORTANT_WARNINGS

- ARC frequently causes underdosing
- Continuous infusion strongly preferred in ICU
- CNS infections may require higher exposure
- Do not reduce dose early in septic shock solely due to renal dysfunction
- Carbapenem stewardship important
- De-escalate when microbiology allows

---

## MICROBIOLOGY_NOTES

Common targeted pathogens:
- ESBL Enterobacterales
- AmpC-risk Enterobacter spp.
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- polymicrobial abdominal infection

Does not reliably cover:
- MRSA
- VRE
- many carbapenemase-producing organisms

---

## ESCALATION_AND_WARNINGS

Escalate to infectious diseases / microbiology consultation if:
- persistent bacteremia
- worsening shock
- persistent subtherapeutic level
- treatment failure
- unclear resistance pattern
- suspected carbapenemase-producing organism
- severe renal dysfunction with uncertain dosing
- CNS infection with poor response

---

## COMMENTS

Continuous infusion meropenem is used to maximize PK/PD target attainment in critically ill patients.

Dose individualization, microbiology-guided de-escalation, and ARC recognition are central stewardship principles.
