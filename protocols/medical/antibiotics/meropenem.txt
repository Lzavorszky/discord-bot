# MEROPENEM

## ALIASES

- meropenem
- mero

---

## INDICATIONS

Common ICU indications:
- severe sepsis
- septic shock
- nosocomial pneumonia
- HAP/VAP
- ESBL-producing Enterobacterales
- severe intraabdominal infection
- CNS infection
- empiric ICU therapy
- AmpC-risk Enterobacter infections
- resistant gram-negative infections

---

## SPECTRUM

Broad-spectrum carbapenem with activity against:
- Enterobacterales
- ESBL-producing organisms
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- anaerobes
- many gram-positive organisms

Limited or unreliable against:
- MRSA
- VRE
- some carbapenem-resistant organisms
- MACI

---

## DOSING_PHILOSOPHY

- Continuous infusion preferred
- PK/PD-driven dosing
- Full-dose therapy recommended in early septic shock
- Higher doses may be required in:
  - ARC
  - CNS infection
  - severe infections

---

## PREPARATION

Standard preparation:
- 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%

Administered:
- as continuous infusion over 24 hours

---

# DOSING_TIERS

| Tier | Typical indication |
|---|---|
| PLUSZ | CNS infection, subtherapeutic level, step-up |
| MAGAS | septic shock first 24 h, ARC suspicion |
| ÁTLAGOS | standard ICU dosing |
| CSÖKKENTETT | severe renal dysfunction |

---

## GFR_BASED_DOSING

| Tier | Daily dose | Administration interval | Pump setting |
|---|---|---|---|
| PLUSZ | 6 g/day | q4h | 12.5 mL/h |
| MAGAS | 4 g/day | q6h | 8.4 mL/h |
| ÁTLAGOS | 3 g/day | q8h | 6.3 mL/h |
| CSÖKKENTETT | 1 g/day | 0.5 g q12h | 4.2 mL/h |
| CRRT | 3 g/day | q8h | 6.3 mL/h |

---

## PLUSZ_DOSE

Used for:
- CNS infections
- persistent subtherapeutic levels
- step-up dosing

Dose:
- 6 g/day

---

## HIGH_DOSE

Used for:
- septic shock empiric therapy
- first 24 hours
- GFR >90 until TDM result available

Dose:
- 4 g/day

---

## STANDARD_DOSE

Used for:
- GFR 20–90
- standard ICU infections

Dose:
- 3 g/day

---

## REDUCED_DOSE

Used for:
- GFR <20
- IHD patients

Dose:
- 1 g/day
OR
- 0.5 g q12h

Important:
- due to low daily dose, administration differs from standard regimen

---

## CRRT

Typical CRRT dosing:
- 3 g/day

Assumes:
- standard effluent rate <40 mL/kg/h

---

## IHD

Typical dosing:
- 0.5 g q12h

Requires:
- individualized consideration
- TDM strongly encouraged

---

## TDM

### Availability

- external laboratory
- Semmelweis University

### Sampling

- send morning sample
- during continuous infusion

---

## TDM_INDICATIONS

Routine TDM:
- NOT generally required

Recommended when:
- ARC suspected
- GFR >90 with urine output >2000 mL/day
- severe AKI
- IHD
- CRRT
- difficult-to-control infection
- poor clinical response
- infective endocarditis
- abscess systems
- persistent infection

---

## PK_PD_TARGETS

Target:
- concentration above pathogen MIC

If MIC unknown:
- >8 mg/L generally acceptable

---

## TOXICITY

Known toxic threshold:
- not fully established

Generally considered safe:
- <45 mg/L

If >45 mg/L:
- dose reduction recommended

---

## IMPORTANT_WARNINGS

- ARC frequently causes underdosing
- Continuous infusion strongly preferred in ICU
- CNS infections may require higher exposure
- Do not reduce dose early in septic shock solely due to renal dysfunction

---

## MICROBIOLOGY_NOTES

Common targeted pathogens:
- ESBL Enterobacterales
- AmpC-risk Enterobacter spp.
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- polymicrobial abdominal infection

Not reliable for:
- MRSA
- VRE
- many carbapenemase-producing organisms

---


## COMMENTS

Continuous infusion meropenem is used to maximize PK/PD target attainment in critically ill patients. Dose individualization and microbiology-guided de-escalation are central stewardship principles.
