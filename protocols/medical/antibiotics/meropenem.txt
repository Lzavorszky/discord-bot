# MEROPENEM

## PROTOCOL_NAME

Meropenem continuous infusion ICU dosing protocol

---

## ALIASES

- meropenem
- mero
- MEM
- meronem

---

## INDICATIONS

Common ICU indications:
- severe sepsis
- septic shock
- nosocomial pneumonia
- HAP/VAP
- ESBL-producing Enterobacterales
- severe intraabdominal infection
- CNS infection
- empiric ICU therapy
- AmpC-risk Enterobacter infections
- resistant gram-negative infections

---

## SPECTRUM

Broad-spectrum carbapenem with activity against:
- Enterobacterales
- ESBL-producing organisms
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- anaerobes
- many gram-positive organisms

Limited or unreliable against:
- MRSA
- VRE
- some carbapenem-resistant organisms
- MACI

---

## REQUIRED_CLARIFICATIONS

Before FINAL pathway selection clarify when not already specified:

- What is the infection type/indication?
- Is septic shock/hemodynamic instability present?
- What is the current GFR/eGFR?
- Is the patient on CRRT?
- Is the patient on intermittent hemodialysis (IHD)?
- Is ARC suspected?
- Is CNS infection suspected?
- Is the patient intubated/mechanically ventilated?
- Is pathogen/MIC available?
- Is TDM available or already performed?

If partial dosing guidance can already be provided:
- provide the available dosing guidance first
- then ask only the remaining clarification questions needed for final pathway selection

Do NOT say "not specified" if relevant partial guidance exists in the protocol.

---

## DOSING_PHILOSOPHY

- Continuous infusion preferred
- PK/PD-driven dosing
- Full-dose therapy recommended in early septic shock
- Higher doses may be required in:
  - ARC
  - CNS infection
  - severe infections

---

## PATHWAY_SELECTION

### PLUSZ

Used for:
- CNS infections
- persistent subtherapeutic levels after TDM
- step-up dosing

Typical dose:
- 6 g/day

---

### MAGAS

Used for:
- septic shock empiric therapy
- first 24 hours
- suspected ARC
- GFR >90 until TDM and pathogen MIC available

Typical dose:
- 4 g/day

---

### ÁTLAGOS

Used for:
- standard ICU infections
- GFR 20–90
- clinically stable patients without ARC/shock

Typical dose:
- 3 g/day

---

### CSÖKKENTETT

Used for:
- GFR <20
- severe renal dysfunction without CRRT

Typical dose:
- 1 g/day
OR
- 0.5 g q12h

---

### CRRT

Typical dose:
- 3 g/day

Assumes:
- standard effluent rate <40 mL/kg/h

---

### IHD

Typical dose:
- 0.5 g q12h

Requires:
- individualized consideration
- TDM strongly encouraged

---

## PRIMARY_DOSING

| Tier | Daily dose |
|---|---|
| PLUSZ | 6 g/day |
| MAGAS | 4 g/day |
| ÁTLAGOS | 3 g/day |
| CSÖKKENTETT | 1 g/day |
| CRRT | 3 g/day |

---

## ADMINISTRATION

Preferred administration:
- continuous infusion over 24 hours

Standard preparation:
- 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%

Pump settings:
- PLUSZ:
  - 12.5 mL/h
- MAGAS:
  - 8.4 mL/h
- ÁTLAGOS:
  - 6.3 mL/h
- CSÖKKENTETT:
  - 4.2 mL/h

Reduced-dose regimens may require modified preparation/administration.

## TDM

### AVAILABILITY

- external laboratory
- Semmelweis University

---

### SAMPLING

- send morning sample
- sample during continuous infusion

---

### TDM_INDICATIONS

Routine TDM:
- NOT generally required

Recommended when:
- ARC suspected
- GFR >90 with urine output >2000 mL/day
- severe AKI
- IHD
- CRRT
- difficult-to-control infection
- poor clinical response
- infective endocarditis
- abscess systems
- persistent infection
- obesity with suspected underexposure

---

## PK_PD_TARGETS

Target:
- concentration above pathogen MIC

If MIC unknown:
- >8 mg/L generally acceptable

---

## TOXICITY

Known toxic threshold:
- not fully established

Generally considered safe:
- <45 mg/L

If >45 mg/L:
- dose reduction recommended

---

## IMPORTANT_WARNINGS

- ARC frequently causes underdosing
- Continuous infusion strongly preferred in ICU
- CNS infections may require higher exposure
- Do not reduce dose early in septic shock solely due to renal dysfunction
- Carbapenem stewardship important
- De-escalate when microbiology allows

---

## MICROBIOLOGY_NOTES

Common targeted pathogens:
- ESBL Enterobacterales
- AmpC-risk Enterobacter spp.
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- polymicrobial abdominal infection

Not works for:
- MRSA
- VRE
- many carbapenemase-producing organisms

---

## ESCALATION_AND_WARNINGS

Escalate to infectious diseases / microbiology consultation if:
- persistent bacteremia
- worsening shock
- persistent subtherapeutic level
- treatment failure
- unclear resistance pattern
- suspected carbapenemase-producing organism
- severe renal dysfunction with uncertain dosing
- CNS infection with poor response

---

## COMMENTS

Continuous infusion meropenem is used to maximize PK/PD target attainment in critically ill patients.

Dose individualization, microbiology-guided de-escalation, and ARC recognition are central stewardship principles.
