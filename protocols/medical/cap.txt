COMMUNITY ACQUIRED RESPIRATORY INFECTIONS
CAP / COPD ACUTE EXACERBATION

---

## PROTOCOL_NAME

Community acquired respiratory infection protocol

---

## ALIASES

- CAP
- community acquired pneumonia
- tüdőgyulladás
- pneumonia
- COPD exacerbation
- aspiration pneumonia

---

## REQUIRED_INFORMATION

Required before final pathway selection:
- Is the patient intubated/mechanically ventilated?

If not specified:
- ask this first before selecting CAP pathway.

---

## PREFERRED_INFORMATION

Helpful for pathway selection and individualization:
- Is viral testing positive or negative?
- Is atypical pathogen suggested by history/symptoms?
- Is the patient hospitalized?
- COPD exacerbation present?
- Are nosocomial risk factors present?
- Is BioFire available?

If missing:
- ask after initial pathway guidance when relevant.

---

## MODIFIER_INFORMATION

These may modify treatment pathway:
- intubation/mechanical ventilation
- nosocomial risk factors
- positive viral testing
- atypical pathogen suspicion
- COPD exacerbation
- aspiration event
- BioFire result
- shock/hemodynamic instability

---

## DEFAULT_ANSWER

No default answer defined.

If required pathway information is missing:
- ask for the minimum information needed to choose the correct pathway.
- do NOT list the full CAP treatment tree unless explicitly requested.

For broad pneumonia questions:
- first clarify:
  - outpatient vs hospitalized?
  - intubated/mechanically ventilated?

Then continue pathway selection.

---

## GENERAL_RULES

- Use Biofire Pneumonia PCR where indicated.
- Consider local microbiology guidance.
- Review allergy history and renal function.
- Assess for nosocomial risk factors.

---

## INFLUENZA

Treatment:
- Oseltamivir

Notes:
- Antibiotics only if bacterial superinfection suspected.

---

## ASPIRATION_PNEUMONIA

Treatment:
- Ceftriaxone

Notes:
- Antibiotics only if secondary infection suspected.
- Not immediately after aspiration event itself.

---

## COPD_ACUTE_EXACERBATION

Treatment:
- Ceftriaxone + clarithromycin

Notes:
- Antibiotics only if bacterial superinfection suspected.

---

## COMMUNITY_ACQUIRED_PNEUMONIA

### OUTPATIENT_DISCHARGEABLE_CAP

If viral test positive:
- antibiotics not required

If viral test negative:
- amoxicillin

If atypical pathogen suspected:
- azithromycin

---

### HOSPITALIZED_CAP_NON_INTUBATED

Without nosocomial risk factors:
- ceftriaxone + clarithromycin

With nosocomial risk factors:
- levofloxacin

---

### INTUBATED_CAP

Treatment:
- follow BioFire-based guidance if available
- until results available:
  - ceftriaxone 2 g

Notes:
- consider corticosteroids if shock present
- example:
  - methylprednisolone 0.5 mg/kg

---

## POINT_OF_CARE_TESTING

### OUTPATIENT_DURING_EPIDEMIC_PERIOD

Combined antigen test:
- indicated during epidemic period

GeneXpert four-panel:
- not indicated

Biofire RP2.1:
- not indicated

Urinary antigen test:
- if clinically suspected

Biofire PN:
- not indicated

---

### HOSPITALIZED_DURING_EPIDEMIC_PERIOD

Combined antigen test:
- clinical suspicion + negative rapid test

GeneXpert four-panel:
- pertussis suspicion

Biofire RP2.1:
- atypical pathogen suspicion

Urinary antigen test:
- indicated

Biofire PN:
- not indicated

---

### INTUBATED_DURING_EPIDEMIC_PERIOD

Combined antigen test:
- not indicated

GeneXpert four-panel:
- pertussis suspicion

Biofire RP2.1:
- indicated

Urinary antigen test:
- indicated

Biofire PN:
- indicated

---

## FOLLOW_UP_QUESTIONS

After providing pathway guidance, ask remaining relevant clarification questions:
- viral test positive or negative?
- atypical pathogen suspicion?
- hospitalized?
- COPD exacerbation?
- nosocomial risk factors?
- BioFire available?
- shock/hemodynamic instability?

Do NOT stop after giving partial pathway guidance if further clarification is still clinically relevant.

---

## SAFETY_RULES

- Ask about allergy history.
- Ask about renal function when antibiotic dosing relevant.
- Ask about shock/hemodynamic instability.
- Do not provide definitive medical decisions.
- Escalate severe or unclear cases to senior clinician/ID specialist.
- No patient identifiers.
