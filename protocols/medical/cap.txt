COMMUNITY ACQUIRED RESPIRATORY INFECTIONS
CAP / COPD ACUTE EXACERBATION

GENERAL RULES
- Use Biofire Pneumonia PCR where indicated.
- Consider local microbiology guidance.
- Review allergy history and renal function.
- Ask whether patient is intubated.
- Assess for nosocomial risk factors.

INFLUENZA
Treatment:
- Oseltamivir

Notes:
- Antibiotics only if bacterial superinfection suspected.

ASPIRATION PNEUMONIA
Treatment:
- Ceftriaxone

Notes:
- Antibiotics only if secondary infection suspected.
- Not immediately after aspiration event itself.

COPD ACUTE EXACERBATION
Treatment:
- Ceftriaxone + clarithromycin

Notes:
- Antibiotics only if bacterial superinfection suspected.

COMMUNITY ACQUIRED PNEUMONIA (CAP)

OUTPATIENT / DISCHARGEABLE CAP

If viral test positive:
- Antibiotics not required.

If viral test negative:
- Amoxicillin

If atypical pathogen suspected:
- Azithromycin

HOSPITALIZED CAP (NON-INTUBATED)

Without nosocomial risk factors:
- Ceftriaxone + clarithromycin

With nosocomial risk factors:
- Levofloxacin

INTUBATED CAP

Treatment:
- Follow Biofire-based guidance if available.
- Until results available: ceftriaxone 2 g

Notes:
- Consider corticosteroids if shock present.
- Example:
  methylprednisolone 0.5 mg/kg

POINT-OF-CARE TESTING

OUTPATIENT DURING EPIDEMIC PERIOD
- Combined antigen test:
  indicated during epidemic period
- GeneXpert four-panel:
  not indicated
- Biofire RP2.1:
  not indicated
- Urinary antigen test:
  if clinically suspected
- Biofire PN:
  not indicated

HOSPITALIZED DURING EPIDEMIC PERIOD
- Combined antigen test:
  clinical suspicion + negative rapid test
- GeneXpert four-panel:
  pertussis suspicion
- Biofire RP2.1:
  atypical pathogen suspicion
- Urinary antigen test:
  indicated
- Biofire PN:
  not indicated

INTUBATED / MECHANICALLY VENTILATED DURING EPIDEMIC PERIOD
- Combined antigen test:
  not indicated
- GeneXpert four-panel:
  pertussis suspicion
- Biofire RP2.1:
  indicated
- Urinary antigen test:
  indicated
- Biofire PN:
  indicated

SAFETY RULES FOR CHATBOT
- Ask about allergy history.
- Ask about renal function.
- Ask whether patient is intubated.
- Ask about nosocomial risk factors.
- Ask about shock/hemodynamic instability.
- Do not provide definitive medical decisions.
- Escalate severe or unclear cases to senior clinician/ID specialist.
- No patient identifiers.
