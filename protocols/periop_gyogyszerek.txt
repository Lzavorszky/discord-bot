## DEFAULT_ANSWER

Use this protocol only for perioperative questions about whether a specific medication or medication class should be continued, omitted, or timed around surgery.

If the user does not name a drug or drug class, ask for the medication or medication class.

If the user only names a drug without a perioperative/surgery context, do not answer from this protocol.

## ANSWER_POLICY

This protocol is active only for perioperative, preoperative, operation/procedure, surgery-day, neuraxial, spinal, epidural, or regional anesthesia medication-management questions.

When a medication or medication class is identified, answer only that medication/class entry.

For antithrombotic entries in antiplatelet_perioperative and anticoagulants_perioperative, return the complete entry under the matching heading. Do not summarize it into one timing. Include all available timing categories in the entry: low bleeding risk, high bleeding risk or spinal/epidural timing, epidural catheter rule, and post-puncture/catheter-removal restart timing when present.

If the question asks generally "when to stop" an antithrombotic before surgery and the entry has different low-risk and high-risk/neuraxial timings, return both listed options rather than choosing one.

If a follow-up asks about epidural, spinal, neuraxial, regional anesthesia, catheter, or puncture after an antithrombotic drug was just discussed, answer from the same antithrombotic entry and include the complete regional-anesthesia/catheter timing from that entry.

Do not ask the user to classify the operation, name the surgery type, or decide low versus high bleeding risk before returning an antithrombotic entry. The clinician will read the complete entry and apply the appropriate row.

Do not provide bridging recommendations, thromboembolic risk stratification, or calculations.

## REQUIRED_INFORMATION

Required before answering:
- perioperative/surgery/procedure/neuraxial context, or active perioperative context from the preceding turn
- named medication or medication class, or a clear follow-up modifier such as epidural/spinal/catheter after a named antithrombotic was just discussed

## DEFAULT_QUESTION

Which medication or medication class are you asking about perioperatively?

## PATHWAY_PRIORITY

1. If the question is not perioperative and there is no active perioperative context, do not answer from this protocol.
2. If the question is about steroid stress-dose or chronic perioperative steroid supplementation, use the separate perioperative steroid guide.
3. If the question is about bridging, say bridging is intentionally not included.
4. If a named antithrombotic is matched, return the complete entry under that heading.
5. If a named non-antithrombotic medication/class is matched, return only the short entry for that medication/class.
6. If no medication/class is named, ask DEFAULT_QUESTION.

### cardiovascular_day_of_surgery

PERIOPERATIVE_CONTEXT_REQUIRED: yes.

Beta blockers; beta-blockers; beta blokkolok; metoprolol; bisoprolol; atenolol; carvedilol:
Take/continue on surgery day. Source note: not recommended to stop; perioperative administration reduces perioperative myocardial ischemia. Starting perioperatively is questionable.

Calcium channel blockers; Ca-channel blockers; Ca-csatorna blokkolok; amlodipine; verapamil; diltiazem:
Take/continue on surgery day. Source note: DHP agents, verapamil and diltiazem included; verapamil has demonstrated cardioprotective effect.

ACE inhibitors; ACE gatlok; ARB; angiotensin receptor blockers:
Conditional. Continue in stable heart failure, monotherapy, or difficult-to-control blood pressure. Omit if not heart failure or if used in combination therapy. Source note: intraoperative hypotension risk may increase, but outcome difference not shown.

Alpha-2 agonists; alfa2 agonistak; moxonidine; clonidine; dexmedetomidine:
Take/continue on surgery day. Source note: analgesic effect, reduced stress response, reduced perioperative cardiac events.

Alpha-1 blockers; alfa1 blokkolok; prazosin; doxazosin:
Take/continue on surgery day. Exception: cataract surgery requires discussion with the surgeon.

Nitrates; nitratok:
Conditional. Omit for high-risk surgery.

Diuretics; diuretikumok; furosemide; hydrochlorothiazide; spironolactone:
Conditional, mostly omit because of hypokalemia and hypovolemia risk. Continue if non-compensated heart failure and the patient was already receiving it.

Amiodarone; amiodaron:
Take/continue on surgery day. Source note: given for arrhythmia prevention.

Digoxin:
Take/continue on surgery day. Source note: check level if toxicity suspected.

Trimetazidine; trimetazidin:
Take/continue on surgery day. Source note: exact certainty unclear, but source favors giving.

Statins; statinok:
Take/continue on surgery day. Source note: plaque-stabilizing effect reduces cardiovascular risk.

### insulin_and_glucose_perioperative

PERIOPERATIVE_CONTEXT_REQUIRED: yes.

Do not calculate insulin doses. Return the source instruction only.

Perioperative glucose target:
Target glucose in the perioperative period: 6-12 mmol/L. Preoperative HbA1c target: <8.5% (<69 mmol/mol). HbA1c screening within 3 months is recommended for all patients with diabetes. If HbA1c >8.5%, diabetology assessment is suggested.

Long-acting insulin; glargine; Lantus; Toujeo; detemir; Levemir; degludec; Tresiba:
Day before surgery: reduce dose by 20%. Day of surgery: reduce dose by 20%. Do not calculate the reduced dose.

Mixed insulin; Humalog Mix; isophane mix; Humulin M3; Insuman Comb; Novomix:
Day before surgery and day of surgery: morning half dose, noon omit, evening depends on eating.

Intermediate-acting insulin; NPH; isophane; Humulin N; Insulotard; Insuman Basal:
Day before surgery and day of surgery: morning half dose, noon omit, evening depends on eating.

Rapid-acting insulin; short-acting insulin; glulisine; Apidra; aspart; Fiasp; Novorapid; lispro; Humalog; Liprolog; Actrapid:
Day before surgery: only with meals. Day of surgery: only with meals; omit if fasting.

### antithrombotic_context

PERIOPERATIVE_CONTEXT_REQUIRED: yes.

This section covers perioperative omission timing for antiplatelet and anticoagulant drugs. Bridging instructions are intentionally omitted. Do not recommend bridging from this protocol.

For low bleeding risk procedures, the source states not to omit the antithrombotic drug and no bridging is needed.

For regional anesthesia: the source states high bleeding risk regional anesthesia requires antithrombotic omission; procedures not listed as high risk are low risk and can be punctured while anticoagulated.

Do not calculate CHA2DS2-VASc, thromboembolic risk, bleeding risk, renal categories, or bridging. If timing depends on bleeding risk, neuraxial anesthesia, or renal function and the user did not provide it, return all available table choices for the named drug; do not ask for surgery type or bleeding-risk classification.
