## DEFAULT_ANSWER

### HU

Meropenem gyors térkép
- MAGAS: 4 g/nap - GFR >90
- ÁTLAGOS: 3 g/nap - GFR 20-90, stabil ICU infekció
- CSÖKKENTETT: 1 g/nap vagy 0.5 g q12h - GFR <20, CRRT nélkül
- CRRT: 3 g/nap
- IHD: 0.5 g q12h

Pontosabb választáshoz küldd: GFR/CRRT/IHD.

### EN

Meropenem quick map
- HIGH: 4 g/day - GFR >90
- STANDARD: 3 g/day - GFR 20-90, stable ICU infection
- REDUCED: 1 g/day or 0.5 g q12h - GFR <20, no CRRT
- CRRT: 3 g/day
- IHD: 0.5 g q12h

For more exact selection send: GFR/CRRT/IHD.

## INFO_BLOCKS

### toxicity

This protocol does not contain a detailed meropenem toxicity section.
If toxicity is asked, answer only that toxicity details are not specified in this protocol.
Do not invent adverse-effect lists.

### monitoring

TDM should be considered if poor response to therapy, extremes of renal function, deep-seated infection, or uncertain exposure.
Target should be above pathogen MIC.
This protocol mentions a practical TDM target range of 8-45 mg/L.

### administration

Use continuous infusion over 24h when the selected tier specifies continuous infusion.
Give a loading dose of 1 g at first administration, then start the 24h infusion after the loading dose finishes.
Preparation for standard continuous infusion tiers: 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%.

### renal

Renal function affects tier selection.
The deterministic dosing tier should be selected from SELECTION_RULES and SELECTED_OUTPUTS.
Do not create renal dosing outside the listed tiers.

## RESTRICTED_OUTPUTS

Never provide:
- non-meropenem antibiotic advice from this protocol
- meropenem dosing outside SELECTED_OUTPUTS
- toxicity details not present in INFO_BLOCKS

## SAFETY_RULES

- Do not reduce meropenem dose in the first 48h of starting therapy unless the protocol-selected tier explicitly does so.
- Select the dose only from renal/RRT status.
- CNS infection, first 48h therapy, poor response, renal extremes, deep-seated infection, MIC and TDM are handled by DEFAULT_FOOTER, not by SELECTION_RULES.

## DEFAULT_FOOTER

! In CNS infections use double dose, e.g. 6g/24h
! Do not reduce dose in the first 48hrs of starting therapy
Give a loading dose of 1g at the first administration and start 24hr infusion after loading finishes
Think TDM if poor response to therapy, extremes of renal function (GFR 20- or 90+), deep seated infection. Aim above pathogen MIC (or 8-45mg/L).
