## DEFAULT_ANSWER

### HU

Meropenem — dozírozási összefoglaló
- PLUSZ: 6 g/nap — CNS infekció vagy alacsony szint
- MAGAS: 4 g/nap — septicus shock első 24h, ARC, GFR >90 amíg MIC/TDM nincs
- ÁTLAGOS: 3 g/nap — GFR 20–90
- CSÖKKENTETT: 1 g/nap vagy 0.5 g q12h — GFR <20, CRRT nélkül
- CRRT: 3 g/nap
- IHD: 0.5 g q12h
- Ha kiválasztod melyik, megmondom a pontos pumpa beállíásokat

### EN

Meropenem — quick map
- PLUSZ: 6 g/day — CNS infection or persistent low levels
- MAGAS: 4 g/day — septic shock first 24h, ARC, GFR >90 until MIC/TDM available
- ÁTLAGOS: 3 g/day — GFR 20–90, stable ICU infection
- CSÖKKENTETT: 1 g/day or 0.5 g q12h — GFR <20, no CRRT
- CRRT: 3 g/day
- IHD: 0.5 g q12h
- If you specify dosing I can tell you pump settings


## PATHWAY_PRIORITY

Priority order (first match wins):

1. CNS infection                       -> PLUSZ
2. Persistent subtherapeutic TDM        -> PLUSZ
3. First 24h of septic shock           -> MAGAS
4. ARC suspected                       -> MAGAS
5. GFR >90 until MIC/TDM available     -> MAGAS
6. CRRT                                -> CRRT
7. Intermittent hemodialysis (IHD)     -> IHD
8. GFR <20 without CRRT                -> CSÖKKENTETT
9. GFR 20-90, stable ICU infection     -> ÁTLAGOS
10. Broad / under-specified request    -> MAGAS as empiric default

## DOSING_TIERS

TIER: PLUSZ
DOSE: 6 g/day
WHEN: CNS infection; persistent low TDM; step-up dosing after subtherapeutic TDM
ADMIN: continuous infusion over 24h
PREP: 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
PUMP: 12.5 mL/h

TIER: MAGAS
DOSE: 4 g/day
WHEN: septic shock empiric therapy; first 24h of septic shock; suspected ARC; GFR >90 until TDM and pathogen MIC available; under-specified broad meropenem dosing question
ADMIN: continuous infusion over 24h
PREP: 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
PUMP: 8.4 mL/h

TIER: ÁTLAGOS
DOSE: 3 g/day
WHEN: standard ICU infection; GFR 20-90; clinically stable patient without ARC/shock/CNS infection
ADMIN: continuous infusion over 24h
PREP: 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
PUMP: 6.3 mL/h

TIER: CSÖKKENTETT
DOSE: 1 g/day OR 0.5 g q12h
WHEN: GFR <20; severe renal dysfunction without CRRT
ADMIN: continuous infusion possible; reduced-dose regimens may require modified preparation; intermittent dosing possible
PREP: individualized / modified preparation may be required
PUMP: 4.2 mL/h

TIER: CRRT
DOSE: 3 g/day
WHEN: CRRT; standard effluent rate <40 mL/kg/h
ADMIN: continuous infusion over 24h
PREP: 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
PUMP: 6.3 mL/h

TIER: IHD
DOSE: 0.5 g q12h
WHEN: intermittent hemodialysis
ADMIN: individualized consideration; intermittent dosing may be preferred
PREP: individualized
PUMP: not applicable / individualized

## DEFAULT_FOOTER
! In CNS infections use double dose, e.g. 6g/24h
! Do not reduce dose in the first 48hrs of starting therapy
Give a loading dose of 1g at the first administration and start 24hr infusion after loading finishes
Think TDM if poor response to therapy, extremes of renal function (GFR 20- or 90+), deep seated infection. Aim above pathogen MIC (or 8-45mg/L)
