## ANSWER_POLICY

Can default dosing be given if some information is missing?
- yes

This is an open default dosing protocol.

If the user asks a broad or under-specified meropenem question, for example:
- mero?
- meropenem?
- meropenem dose?
- meropenem dózis?
- mennyi meropenem?
- meronem?

Then:
- provide the DEFAULT_ANSWER in the user's language if available
- include the quick dosing tier structure
- do NOT refuse dosing solely because indication or renal function is missing
- label the default as empiric / not individualized
- after the answer, ask briefly for preferred information needed to individualize dosing

If the user provides modifiers, select the dosing tier using PATHWAY_PRIORITY and DOSING_TIERS.

Do NOT:
- invent non-protocol dosing
- omit continuous infusion
- omit renal replacement therapy if mentioned
- recommend renal dose reduction in the first 24h of septic shock solely because of renal dysfunction unless IHD/anuria/toxicity dominates

## DEFAULT_ANSWER

### HU

Meropenem — dozírozási összefoglaló
- PLUSZ: 6 g/nap — CNS infekció vagy alacsony szint
- MAGAS: 4 g/nap — septicus shock első 24h, ARC, GFR >90 amíg MIC/TDM nincs
- ÁTLAGOS: 3 g/nap — GFR 20–90
- CSÖKKENTETT: 1 g/nap vagy 0.5 g q12h — GFR <20, CRRT nélkül
- CRRT: 3 g/nap
- IHD: 0.5 g q12h
- Ha kiválasztod melyik, megmondom a pontos pumpa beállíásokat

### EN

Meropenem — quick map
- PLUSZ: 6 g/day — CNS infection or persistent low levels
- MAGAS: 4 g/day — septic shock first 24h, ARC, GFR >90 until MIC/TDM available
- ÁTLAGOS: 3 g/day — GFR 20–90, stable ICU infection
- CSÖKKENTETT: 1 g/day or 0.5 g q12h — GFR <20, no CRRT
- CRRT: 3 g/day
- IHD: 0.5 g q12h
- If you specify dosing I can tell you pump settings


## PATHWAY_PRIORITY

Priority order (first match wins):

1. CNS infection                       -> PLUSZ
2. Persistent subtherapeutic TDM        -> PLUSZ
3. First 24h of septic shock           -> MAGAS
4. ARC suspected                       -> MAGAS
5. GFR >90 until MIC/TDM available     -> MAGAS
6. CRRT                                -> CRRT
7. Intermittent hemodialysis (IHD)     -> IHD
8. GFR <20 without CRRT                -> CSÖKKENTETT
9. GFR 20-90, stable ICU infection     -> ÁTLAGOS
10. Broad / under-specified request    -> MAGAS as empiric default

## DOSING_TIERS

TIER: PLUSZ
DOSE: 6 g/day
WHEN: CNS infection; persistent low TDM; step-up dosing after subtherapeutic TDM
ADMIN: continuous infusion over 24h
PREP: 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
PUMP: 12.5 mL/h

TIER: MAGAS
DOSE: 4 g/day
WHEN: septic shock empiric therapy; first 24h of septic shock; suspected ARC; GFR >90 until TDM and pathogen MIC available; under-specified broad meropenem dosing question
ADMIN: continuous infusion over 24h
PREP: 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
PUMP: 8.4 mL/h

TIER: ÁTLAGOS
DOSE: 3 g/day
WHEN: standard ICU infection; GFR 20-90; clinically stable patient without ARC/shock/CNS infection
ADMIN: continuous infusion over 24h
PREP: 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
PUMP: 6.3 mL/h

TIER: CSÖKKENTETT
DOSE: 1 g/day OR 0.5 g q12h
WHEN: GFR <20; severe renal dysfunction without CRRT
ADMIN: continuous infusion possible; reduced-dose regimens may require modified preparation; intermittent dosing possible
PREP: individualized / modified preparation may be required
PUMP: 4.2 mL/h

TIER: CRRT
DOSE: 3 g/day
WHEN: CRRT; standard effluent rate <40 mL/kg/h
ADMIN: continuous infusion over 24h
PREP: 1 g meropenem in 50 mL NaCl 0.9%
PUMP: 6.3 mL/h

TIER: IHD
DOSE: 0.5 g q12h
WHEN: intermittent hemodialysis
ADMIN: individualized consideration; intermittent dosing may be preferred
PREP: individualized
PUMP: not applicable / individualized

## TREATMENT_PATHWAYS


### SPECTRUM

Broad-spectrum carbapenem with activity against:
- Enterobacterales
- ESBL-producing organisms
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- anaerobes
- many gram-positive organisms

Limited or unreliable against:
- MRSA
- VRE
- some carbapenem-resistant organisms
- MACI


### UNDER_SPECIFIED_DOSING_REQUESTS
provide default answer

### TDM

#### AVAILABILITY

- external laboratory
- Semmelweis University

#### SAMPLING

- send morning sample
- sample during continuous infusion

#### TDM_INDICATIONS

Routine TDM:
- NOT generally required

Recommended when:
- ARC suspected
- GFR >90 with urine output >2000 mL/day
- severe AKI
- IHD
- CRRT
- difficult-to-control infection
- poor clinical response
- infective endocarditis
- abscess systems
- persistent infection

### PK_PD_TARGETS

Target:
- concentration above pathogen MIC

If MIC unknown:
- >8 mg/L generally acceptable

## SAFETY_NOTES

### TOXICITY

Known toxic threshold:
- not fully established

Generally considered safe:
- <45 mg/L

If >45 mg/L:
- dose reduction recommended

### IMPORTANT_WARNINGS

- ARC frequently causes underdosing
- Continuous infusion strongly preferred in ICU
- CNS infections may require higher exposure
- Do not reduce dose early in septic shock solely due to renal dysfunction
- Carbapenem stewardship important
- De-escalate when microbiology allows

### MICROBIOLOGY_NOTES

Common targeted pathogens:
- ESBL Enterobacterales
- AmpC-risk Enterobacter spp.
- Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- polymicrobial abdominal infection

Does not reliably cover:
- MRSA
- VRE
- many carbapenemase-producing organisms
- MACI

### ESCALATION_AND_WARNINGS

## DEFAULT_FOOTER
Think TDM if poor response to therapy, extremes of renal function, high MIC
