## DEFAULT_ANSWER

### HU

Hasuri infekciok gyors terkep:
- Hazabocsathato beteg, pl. diverticulosis: amoxicillin/klavulansav. Penicillin allergia / alternativa a forras szerint: cefixim + metronidazol.
- Hospitalizalando beteg, source kontrollal, pl. megoldott perforacio vagy ileus: ceftriaxone + metronidazol. Megjegyzes: szeptikus sokk eseten is.
- Komplex nosocomialis hasuri fertozes, pl. reoperaciot igenylo varratelegtelenseg: meropenem. Intraoperativ minta BioFire JI PCR alapjan szukseg szerint eszkalacio, nem deeszkalacio; PCR es konvencionalis tenyesztes alapjan celzott kezeles.
- Pancreatitis: prezentaciokor antibiotikum nem szukseges, csak szeptikus szovodmeny eseten.
- Varix verzes profilaxis: ceftriaxone.
- Spontan bakterialis peritonitis: ceftriaxone.
- Splenectomia profilaxis: fenoximetilpenicillin 2x1 ME.
- Clostridium difficile: kezelesi reszletekhez hasznald a C. difficile protokoll kezeles chunkjat.

Pontosabb valasztashoz kuldd a helyzetet: hazaengedheto, hospitalizalt/source kontroll, komplex nosocomialis, pancreatitis, varix verges, SBP, splenectomia vagy C. difficile.

### EN

Intra-abdominal infection quick map:
- Dischargeable patient, e.g. diverticulosis: amoxicillin/clavulanate. Source alternative / penicillin allergy column: cefixime + metronidazole.
- Hospitalized patient with source control, e.g. solved perforation or ileus: ceftriaxone + metronidazole. Source note: also in septic shock.
- Complex nosocomial intra-abdominal infection, e.g. anastomotic leak requiring reoperation: meropenem. Intraoperative BioFire JI PCR may support escalation if needed, not de-escalation; use PCR and conventional culture for targeted therapy.
- Pancreatitis: no antibiotic at presentation, only if septic complication is present.
- Variceal bleeding prophylaxis: ceftriaxone.
- Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis: ceftriaxone.
- Splenectomy prophylaxis: phenoxymethylpenicillin 2x1 MU.
- Clostridium difficile: for treatment details use the treatment chunk in the C. difficile protocol.

For exact selection send the context: dischargeable, hospitalized/source control, complex nosocomial, pancreatitis, variceal bleeding, SBP, splenectomy, or C. difficile.

## INFO_BLOCKS

### biofire_ji_pcr_note

The source says BioFire JI PCR is potentially usable for abdominal infection.
For complex nosocomial abdominal infection, it says intraoperative BioFire JI PCR may be used for escalation if needed, not de-escalation.
Do not de-escalate below meropenem in nosocomial abdominal infection from JI PCR alone; wait for conventional culture for narrowing.

### pancreatitis

The source states that pancreatitis does not need antibiotics at presentation.
Antibiotics are used only if septic complication is present.
