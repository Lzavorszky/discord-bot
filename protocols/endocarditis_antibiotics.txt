## DEFAULT_ANSWER

### HU

Empiric infective endocarditis treatment - starting options:
- NVE / late PVE: ampicillin 12 g/day IV + ceftriaxone 4 g/day IV/IM in 2 doses + gentamicin 3 mg/kg
- Penicillin allergy: cefazolin 6 g/day IV + vancomycin 2 g load + 2 g/24h (see local guideline) + gentamicin 3 mg/kg
- Early PVE (<12 months after surgery): cefazolin 6 g/day IV + vancomycin 2 g load + 2 g/24h (see local guideline) + gentamicin 3 mg/kg

If a pathogen is known, send the pathogen name or susceptibility, for example MSSA, MRSA, Enterococcus, VRE, or Streptococcus.

### EN

Empiric infective endocarditis treatment - starting options:
- NVE / late PVE: ampicillin 12 g/day IV + ceftriaxone 4 g/day IV/IM in 2 doses + gentamicin 3 mg/kg
- Penicillin allergy: cefazolin 6 g/day IV + vancomycin 2 g load + 2 g/24h (see local guideline) + gentamicin 3 mg/kg
- Early PVE (<12 months after surgery): cefazolin 6 g/day IV + vancomycin 2 g load + 2 g/24h (see local guideline) + gentamicin 3 mg/kg

If a pathogen is known, send the pathogen name or susceptibility, for example MSSA, MRSA, Enterococcus, VRE, or Streptococcus.

## INFO_BLOCKS

### scope

This protocol covers simplified adult empiric and selected targeted antibiotic pathways for infective endocarditis.
It intentionally does not cover culture-negative endocarditis, fungal endocarditis, OPAT, oral step-down, surgery, diagnosis, or general endocarditis principles.

### pathogen_alias_policy

MSSA and MRSA should route as Staphylococcus aureus susceptibility variants.
Enterococcus without resistance information should show beta-lactam-sensitive, beta-lactam-resistant-not-VRE, and VRE options.
Staphylococcus aureus without resistance information should show MSSA and MRSA options.
PVE without timing should show both late and early PVE empiric options.

## RESTRICTED_OUTPUTS

Never provide:
- culture-negative endocarditis regimens from this protocol
- fungal endocarditis regimens from this protocol
- OPAT or oral step-down advice from this protocol
- pathogen-specific therapy for pathogens not listed in SELECTED_OUTPUTS
- paediatric dosing
- detailed renal adjustment, TDM adjustment, or toxicity management from this protocol
- allergy severity classification from this protocol

If the pathogen-specific guideline is not available, say so and refer to specialist/full guideline.
For detailed drug dosing beyond the short starting dose strings, use linked drug protocols where available.

## SAFETY_RULES

- This pathway is intentionally simplified for starting treatment.
- If no selection details are supplied, show DEFAULT_ANSWER.
- If pathogen is supplied, targeted pathogen outputs have priority over empiric outputs.
- Resistance information is optional; when missing, show the available resistance options rather than forcing a question.
- NVE/PVE and PVE timing are optional; when PVE timing is missing, show both early and late PVE options.
- Penicillin allergy is optional and not severity-classified here; show the specified cefazolin-containing pathway and append the footer.
- Rifampicin is added only after bacteremia clears.
- ID advice should always be sought in daytime.
