## DEFAULT_ANSWER

### HU

UTI gyors terkep
- tunetmentes bacteriuria: nem kell kezelni
- nem komplikalt hugyuti fertozes: fosfomycin vagy nitrofurantoin
- komplikalt hugyuti fertozes, hazaengedheto: cefuroxime
- komplikalt hugyuti fertozes, hospitalizalt: ceftriaxone
- komplikalt hugyuti fertozes, hospitalizalt + nosocomialis rizikofaktor: ertapenem

Diagnosztika: vizelet tenyesztes es kemia; kateteres betegnnel katetercsere utan friss vizeletbol kuldj mintat. Szisztemas fertozes eseten ket par hemokultura. Merlegeld kismedencei ultrahang, CT es urologus szuksegesseget.

Pontosabb valasztashoz kuldd: tunetmentes bacteriuria / nem komplikalt / komplikalt, hazaengedheto vagy hospitalizalt, van-e nosocomialis rizikofaktor.

### EN

UTI quick map
- asymptomatic bacteriuria: do not treat
- uncomplicated urinary tract infection: fosfomycin or nitrofurantoin
- complicated urinary tract infection, dischargeable: cefuroxime
- complicated urinary tract infection, hospitalized: ceftriaxone
- complicated urinary tract infection, hospitalized + nosocomial risk factor: ertapenem

Diagnostics: urine culture and urine chemistry; in catheterized patients send a fresh urine sample after catheter change. If systemic infection is present, send two blood-culture pairs. Consider pelvic ultrasound, CT, and urology involvement.

For more exact selection send: asymptomatic bacteriuria / uncomplicated / complicated, dischargeable or hospitalized, and whether nosocomial risk factor is present.

## INFO_BLOCKS

### diagnostics

Diagnostic checklist:
- send urine culture and urine chemistry
- in catheterized patients, change catheter first and send the sample from fresh urine, not from the old catheter
- if systemic infection is present, send two blood-culture pairs
- consider pelvic ultrasound, CT, and urology involvement

### catheter_associated_uti

Severe ICU-acquired CA-UTI is rare.
If CA-UTI is suspected, catheter change and fresh urine sampling are required before sending urine culture/chemistry.
Catheter status alone should not select an antimicrobial without dischargeable/hospitalized and nosocomial-risk context.

### syndrome_notes

Complicated UTI examples in the source include pyelonephritis, catheter-associated infection, and prostatitis.
UTI does not provide antimicrobial dosing.
If dosing is requested, use the linked antimicrobial protocol when available.

### allergy_and_safety

Ask about allergy history.
Ask about renal function when antibiotic dosing is requested because dosing belongs to linked drug protocols.
Escalate severe or unclear cases to senior clinician / ID specialist.
No patient identifiers.

## RESTRICTED_OUTPUTS

Never provide:
- antimicrobial doses from UTI
- renal dose adjustment from UTI
- fosfomycin, nitrofurantoin, cefuroxime, ceftriaxone, or ertapenem dosing unless a linked protocol is active
- molecular-panel testing pathway from UTI
- molecular-panel interpretation from UTI
- antibiotic selection for catheter-associated status alone without dischargeable/hospitalized and nosocomial-risk context
- catheter change as the uncomplicated UTI treatment pathway
- antibiotics for asymptomatic bacteriuria

For dosing requests:
- use LINKS
- if the linked target protocol is missing, say the target_missing_behavior sentence

For info requests:
- answer only from INFO_BLOCKS
- do not add dosing

## SAFETY_RULES

- UTI selects treatment pathway only; it does not dose drugs.
- Asymptomatic bacteriuria has highest priority and selects no treatment.
- Nosocomial risk modifies hospitalized complicated UTI and selects ertapenem.
- Catheter-associated suspicion triggers catheter change and fresh urine sampling guidance; treatment selection still needs dischargeable/hospitalized and nosocomial-risk context.
- No molecular-panel pathway exists in this protocol.
- After a pathway names an antimicrobial, keep it as last_selected_item so a follow-up dosing request can activate LINKS.
- If multiple antimicrobials are selected, and the user asks "dose?", ask which one unless the user named one.
- If the user supplies GFR/renal data during a UTI pathway, store it and transfer it through LINKS; do not use it inside UTI.
