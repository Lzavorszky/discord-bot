## DEFAULT_ANSWER

### HU

Meropenem dosing table:

| Tier | When | Daily dose | Administration |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Normal | GFR 20+ | 3 g/day | 1 g/50 mL, 6.3 mL/h |
| Severe AKI | GFR <20 or IHD | 1 g/day | 0.5 g/50 mL, 4.2 mL/h |
| CRRT | CRRT | 3 g/day | 1 g/50 mL, 6.3 mL/h |
| Step-up dose | low levels or CNS infection | 6 g/day | 1 g/50 mL, 12.5 mL/h |

Reduced-dose preparation: dissolve 1 g in 20 mL NaCl 0.9%, withdraw 10 mL, dilute to 50 mL for a 0.5 g/50 mL syringe.

## INFO_BLOCKS

### source_scope

This is an open dosing-table protocol. Broad drug requests may return DEFAULT_ANSWER. If GFR, CRRT, or IHD is supplied, deterministic selection may return the matching tier.


### administration

Administration is intentionally compact for mobile viewing: dose/dilution and pump rate are kept in one line. Do not invent alternative dilutions, infusion durations, or pump rates.

## RESTRICTED_OUTPUTS

Never provide:
- meropenem dosing outside the listed table rows
- Step-up dose unless CNS infection or low exposure is explicitly present
- alternative antibiotics, duration, toxicity management, or indication advice from this protocol

## SAFETY_RULES

- For drugs without a separate anuria/IHD row, IHD maps to Severe AKI.
- If renal data conflict, IHD has highest priority, then CRRT, then numeric GFR.
- Use Step-up dose only when this protocol explicitly lists it and the user provides CNS infection or low exposure/TDM context.
