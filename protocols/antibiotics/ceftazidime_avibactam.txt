| Tier | When | Daily dose | Administration | Preparation | Pump |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|

| Anuria/IHD | anuria or IHD | 1.25 g/day | every 48 h according to source reduced column | reduced-dose preparation note below | 2.1 mL/h |
| CRRT | CRRT | 7.5 g/day | one 2/0.5 g syringe over 8 h | 2/0.5 g in 50 mL NaCl 0.9% -> 40 mg/mL | 6.3 mL/h |
| Step-up dose | only if TDM suggests low levels | 10 g/day | one 2/0.5 g syringe over 6 h | 2/0.5 g in 50 mL NaCl 0.9% -> 40 mg/mL | 8.4 mL/h |

Reduced-dose preparation note from source: dissolve the 2.5 g ampoule in 20 mL NaCl 0.9%, withdraw half (10 mL), dilute further to 50 mL, giving a 1 g/0.24 g/50 mL syringe over 24 h. Label the remaining ampoule with date/name, refrigerate, and prepare the second 50 mL syringe after 24 h.

The uploaded table does not define numeric GFR cutoffs for Normal, severe renal dysfunction, or Step-up dose.
