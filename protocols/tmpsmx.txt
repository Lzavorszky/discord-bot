TMP/SMX dosing needs:
- indication
- body weight in kg
- renal function: GFR/eGFR/CrCl or CRRT/IHD

Indication groups:
1. PCP/PJP treatment, Stenotrophomonas BSI, Nocardia -> HIGH_DOSE
2. Severe/CNS/bone-joint/refractory/ICU infection -> MODERATE_DOSE
3. Standard susceptible infection or oral step-down -> STANDARD_DOSE
4. Prophylaxis in immunosuppressed patient -> PROPHYLAXIS

### INDICATION_RULES

RULE: HIGH_DOSE
  IF_INDICATION_CONTAINS: pcp, pjp, pneumocystis, pneumocystis jirovecii, steno, stenotrophomonas, stenotrophomonas maltophilia, bloodstream infection, bsi, bacteraemia, bacteremia, nocardia
  TARGET_TMP_MG_KG_DAY: 15-20

RULE: MODERATE_DOSE
  IF_INDICATION_CONTAINS: severe, cns, meningitis, brain abscess, bone, joint, osteomyelitis, septic arthritis, refractory, icu, critically ill, deep-seated, severe ssti, severe skin soft tissue
  TARGET_TMP_MG_KG_DAY: 8-10

RULE: STANDARD_DOSE
  IF_INDICATION_CONTAINS: standard, susceptible, non-septic, nonseptic, oral step-down, stepdown, step down, uncomplicated, susceptible infection
  TARGET_TMP_MG_KG_DAY: fixed practical dose

RULE: PROPHYLAXIS
  IF_INDICATION_CONTAINS: prophylaxis, prophylactic, pcp prophylaxis, pjp prophylaxis, immunosuppressed, immunosuppression, hematology, haematology, transplant
  TARGET_TMP_MG_KG_DAY: prophylactic fixed dose

### RENAL_RULES

  IF: crrt == true

  COMMENT: full dose generally required

## INFO_BLOCKS

### toxicity

Potential adverse effects listed in this protocol:
- hyperkalemia
- hyponatremia
- AKI
- crystalluria
- pseudo-creatinine elevation
- leukopenia
- thrombocytopenia
- pancytopenia
- megaloblastic anemia
- benign liver enzyme elevation
- SJS/TEN
- DRESS
- nausea
- vomiting

High-risk toxicity situations:
- renal dysfunction
- high-dose treatment
- prolonged treatment
- elderly patient
- ACE inhibitor / ARB / spironolactone / other hyperkalemia risk

Do not provide dosing from this block.

### monitoring

Monitor:
- potassium
- creatinine
- sodium
- CBC

Check potassium and creatinine within 48h.

### renal

Renal function is required before TMP/SMX treatment dosing.
GFR >30 or CRRT: full dose category.
GFR 15-30: approximately 50% treatment dose category.
GFR <15 without CRRT: avoid if alternative exists; if unavoidable, individualized ID/pharmacy decision.
IHD: individualized dosing, dose after dialysis, ID/pharmacy decision recommended.

Do not select a treatment dose from this block; use SELECTION_RULES and SELECTED_OUTPUTS.

### formulation

1 ampoule contains 80 mg trimethoprim and 400 mg sulfamethoxazole.
1 tablet contains 80 mg trimethoprim and 400 mg sulfamethoxazole.
Enteral administration uses the same dose as IV if absorption is expected.

### weight

Use actual body weight.
If BMI >30, use adjusted body weight if available.
If adjusted body weight is not available and obesity is relevant, ask for height or adjusted body weight.
If body weight >100 kg, individualized dosing decision is required and ID/pharmacy consultation is recommended.
If body weight is between table rows, use the closest practical dose that stays within or near the selected TMP mg/kg/day target.
Avoid underdosing severe infection.

## RESTRICTED_OUTPUTS

Never provide:
- TMP/SMX treatment dosing unless indication, body_weight_kg, and renal_function are available
- empiric/default TMP/SMX treatment dose
- doses outside SELECTED_OUTPUTS
- full dosing tables unless explicitly requested
- toxicity management not present in INFO_BLOCKS

For info requests:
- answer only from INFO_BLOCKS
- do not include a treatment dose
- do not ask for missing dosing inputs unless the user also asks for dosing

## SAFETY_RULES

- In severe infections, do not underdose.
- ID consultation is recommended for unusual pathogens, CNS infection, Nocardia, severe renal dysfunction, CRRT/IHD, or treatment failure.
- If GFR <15 without CRRT, avoid TMP/SMX if alternative exists.
- If IHD, dose after dialysis and individualize with ID/pharmacy.
