## ANSWER_POLICY

Can default dosing be given if some information is missing?
- no

TMP/SMX dosing depends strongly on:
- indication
- renal function
- body weight

Do NOT provide dosing before required information is available.

Do NOT provide empiric/default dosing.

Do NOT provide full dosing tables unless explicitly requested.

Instead:
- ask for the required information first
- then provide the matching dosing recommendation.

## REQUIRED_INFORMATION

Required before dosing:
- indication/infection type
- current renal function/GFR
- body weight

Examples of common indications:
- PCP/PJP prophylaxis
- Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia
- invasive Stenotrophomonas infection
- Nocardia infection
- skin/soft tissue infection
- bone/joint infection
- oral step-down treatment

If indication unclear:
- ask for suspected pathogen or infection type.

If body weight not provided:
- ask for current body weight.

If renal function not provided:
- ask for:
  - GFR/eGFR
  - CRRT?
  - intermittent hemodialysis (IHD)?

Do NOT provide dosing before all required information is available.

## PATHWAY_PRIORITY

Priority order (first match wins):

1. PCP/PJP treatment, invasive Stenotrophomonas, Nocardia   -> HIGH_DOSE (15-20 mg/kg/day TMP)
2. Severe / CNS / bone-joint / refractory / ICU infection   -> MODERATE_DOSE (8-10 mg/kg/day TMP)
3. Standard susceptible infection / oral step-down           -> STANDARD_DOSE (2 x 2 amp)
4. Prophylaxis in immunosuppressed patient                   -> PROPHYLAXIS

Apply renal adjustment (RENAL_DOSING) after selecting tier.
Apply weight rules (WEIGHT_RULES) for dose calculation.

## TREATMENT_PATHWAYS

### PROPHYLAXIS

#### PCP prophylaxis in immunosuppressed patients

Recommended regimen:
- 1 tablet daily
OR
- 1 tablet three times weekly

Recommendation:
- discuss with hematologist

### STANDARD_DOSE

Used for:
- susceptible organism infections
- non-septic patients
- oral step-down treatment

Typical dosing:
- 2 x 2 ampoules IV

### MODERATE_DOSE

Used for:
- severe infections
- treatment-refractory infections
- CNS infections
- skin/soft tissue infections
- bone/joint infections
- critically ill patients

Target dose:
- 8–10 mg/kg/day TMP component

### HIGH_DOSE

Used for:
- Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia
- invasive Stenotrophomonas infection
- Nocardia infection

Target dose:
- 15–20 mg/kg/day TMP component

### FORMULATION

1 tablet or 1 ampoule contains:
- 80 mg trimethoprim
- 400 mg sulfamethoxazole

### ENTERAL_ADMINISTRATION

Enteral administration:
- same dose as IV

If clinically improving:
- oral conversion recommended

### WEIGHT_RULES

- Use actual body weight
- If BMI >30:
  - use adjusted body weight (ABW)
- >100 kg:
  - individualized dosing decision required

### RENAL_DOSING

#### General principles

| Renal function | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| GFR >30 | no adjustment |
| GFR 15–30 | give 50% dose |
| GFR <15 | avoid if alternative exists |
| CRRT | full dose |

### ICU_NOTES

- High-dose therapy commonly needed in ICU infections
- Particularly useful for:
  - Stenotrophomonas
  - Pneumocystis
  - Nocardia

In severe infections:
- do not underdose

### SPECIAL_POPULATIONS

#### Obesity
- use ABW if BMI >30

#### CRRT
- full dosing generally required

#### IHD
- dose after dialysis

## SAFETY_NOTES

### MICROBIOLOGY_NOTES

Particularly important pathogens:
- Pneumocystis jirovecii
- Stenotrophomonas maltophilia
- Nocardia spp.

High-dose therapy frequently required in:
- invasive disease
- ICU patients
- pulmonary infections

### TOXICITY

Potential adverse effects:

#### Hematologic
- leukopenia
- thrombocytopenia
- pancytopenia
- megaloblastic anemia

#### Electrolyte
- hyperkalemia
- hyponatremia

#### Renal
- AKI
- crystalluria
- pseudo-creatinine elevation

#### Hepatic
- benign liver enzyme elevation

#### Hypersensitivity
- SJS/TEN
- DRESS
- idiosyncratic reactions

#### Gastrointestinal
- nausea
- vomiting

### IMPORTANT_WARNINGS

- Hyperkalemia risk significant
- Toxicity risk increases substantially with renal dysfunction
- Avoid in severe renal failure if alternatives exist
- Monitor:
  - CBC
  - creatinine
  - potassium
  - sodium

### COMMENTS

TMP/SMX dosing schemes are heterogeneous and not fully evidence-based. Clinical judgment and infectious diseases consultation may be necessary in severe or unusual infections.
