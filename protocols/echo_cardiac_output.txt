## DEFAULT_ANSWER

Provide LVOT VTI and LVOT diameter. If heart rate is also supplied, this calculator returns cardiac output.

## SELECTION_RULES

selection_mode: none

Unit conversion rules:
- LVOT diameter may be supplied in mm or cm.
- LVOT VTI may be supplied in mm or cm.
- Convert mm to cm by dividing by 10.
- Heart rate is beats per minute.

Calculation rules:
- LVOT_diameter_cm = LVOT_diameter_mm / 10 if supplied in mm
- LVOT_CSA_cm2 = pi * (LVOT_diameter_cm / 2)^2
- Stroke_volume_ml = LVOT_CSA_cm2 * LVOT_VTI_cm
- If heart_rate_bpm is supplied: Cardiac_output_L_min = Stroke_volume_ml * heart_rate_bpm / 1000
- If heart_rate_bpm is not supplied, return stroke volume only.

## SELECTED_OUTPUTS

### calculated_sv

Return LVOT diameter in cm, LVOT CSA in cm2, LVOT VTI in cm, and stroke volume in mL.

### calculated_co

Return LVOT diameter in cm, LVOT CSA in cm2, LVOT VTI in cm, stroke volume in mL, heart rate in bpm, and cardiac output in L/min.

### missing_input

Ask for LVOT diameter and/or LVOT VTI. Heart rate is optional.

### unsupported_units

Ask the user to resend LVOT diameter and LVOT VTI in mm or cm, and heart rate in bpm if cardiac output is requested.

## INFO_BLOCKS

### method_notes

Stroke volume by pulsed-wave Doppler is calculated as cross-sectional area times VTI. The LVOT area is commonly estimated from LVOT diameter using the circular area formula. Cardiac output is stroke volume multiplied by heart rate.

### quality_notes

LVOT diameter error is squared in the formula. The calculation assumes a representative LVOT VTI tracing, appropriate sample-volume placement, and compatible measurement timing.

## RESTRICTED_OUTPUTS

Never provide:
- treatment advice for low or high cardiac output
- shock classification
- valve disease grading
- fluid responsiveness advice
- indexed cardiac output unless BSA is explicitly supplied and the user asks for it
- assumptions for missing LVOT diameter, LVOT VTI, or heart rate

## SAFETY_RULES

- Require LVOT diameter and LVOT VTI before calculating stroke volume.
- Use mm-to-cm conversion when units are explicit.
- If the unit is ambiguous, ask for clarification rather than guessing.
- Calculate cardiac output only when heart rate is supplied.
